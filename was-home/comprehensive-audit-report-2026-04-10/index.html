Introduction

We onboarded Was-Home on January 6th.

When we came in, there was nothing. No sign-up form. No campaigns. No email flows. The entire email list had just 733 subscribers.

We built everything from scratch.

The first sign-up form went live on January 16th. So from the point where we actually started capturing new subscribers, it hasn't even been 3 months yet.

The key revenue-driving flows (Checkout, Browse, Cart) didn't go live until January 23rd and later. The second batch of flows were live by late February.

Also, we can't judge a campaign's performance on the day it's sent. Every campaign keeps generating revenue over the following days as people open, click, and purchase.

Email marketing doesn't work like paid ads, where you may see results the next day. It takes time to build a list, warm up the audience, and let the automations do their job. The minimum is 3 months before you start seeing real traction, and we are already getting traction after these three months of working together. You will see the improvements below.

Initiatives

Here's what we're actively doing to push performance further:

Weekly Tracking: We track everything weekly through a Unified Dashboard so nothing slips through the cracks.

Continuous AB Testing: We're testing everything. Sign-up forms, campaigns, flows. We are not being static. We are optimizing based on the data.

Increased Campaign Frequency: Campaigns were generating around $1,000 to $1,500 per month. We ramped up the frequency, and in just the first 10 days of April, campaigns have already generated $9,000.

Alia Sign-Up Form: We introduced a new sign-up form to increase the sign-up rate. More subscribers means more people receiving campaigns, which means more revenue.
